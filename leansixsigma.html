Lean Six Sigma Project

Reducing Service Turnaround Time in Customer Support

Challenge

The customer support division of a telecom client faced long resolution times (36 hours/ticket), high rework, and declining CSAT.

Approach – DMAIC Framework

  • Define: Mapped end-to-end workflow using SIPOC; captured Voice of Customer to define goals.
  • Measure: Collected 3 months of ticket data; established baseline metrics and visualized Pareto charts.
  • Analyze: Identified inefficiencies using Fishbone and 5 Whys; confirmed correlation between handoffs and delays.
  • Improve: Implemented Quick Resolution Path, checklist, and workflow redesign; piloted changes.
  • Control: Power BI dashboards with SLA alerts, monthly audits, and SOP updates.

Results

MetricBeforeAfterImprovement
Avg Resolution Time36 hrs24 hrs33% ↓
Rework Rate22%10%55% ↓
First Contact Resolution65%80%+23%
CSAT82%90%+8 pts

Reflection

This project reaffirmed that Lean Six Sigma isn’t just about reducing defects — it’s about empowering people and building clarity into processes.
